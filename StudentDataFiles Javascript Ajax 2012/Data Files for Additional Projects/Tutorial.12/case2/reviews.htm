A Classic

Pros Comfortable, Fast, Handles Well, Responsive
Best Uses Commuting, Touring, Serious Training
Describe Yourself Avid Cyclist
Bottom Line Yes, I would recommend this to a friend

This is a classic touring bike. The geometry is more upright than standard tourers, but it's easy to adjust to. I found the steering to be very responsive, even when loaded, which was a pleasant surprise. All the components are good quality. I use this bike for commuting as well as weekend tours, mostly on country roads.

I'm looking forward to doing a tour around Lake Michigan with this bike next spring. I expect it to perform like a champ.

Paul M from Owatonna, MN; Sep. 4, 2012

Good Touring Bike

Pros Poor Components
Best Uses Commuting
Describe Yourself Avid Cyclist
Bottom Line No, I would not recommend this to a friend

I needed a good road bike for Colorado and I thought the Tour 250 was the answer. While it is a durable bike for pulling trailers or hanging panniers, I think there are several areas that need attention. First - a more upright posture or include an adjustable stem as standard. Second - the cassette should be 11-34 instead of 11-32. Three - they should offer/provide a front rack instead of having to find one that works.

The new model geometry looks better than previous editions offered by Pedal Mart, so perhaps some other improvements are in the works. At this point though, while I think this is a good bike, I can't recommend it over similarly priced competition.

Kevin D from Rifle, CO; July 31, 2012
