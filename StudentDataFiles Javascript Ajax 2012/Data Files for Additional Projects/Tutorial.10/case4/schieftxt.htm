Tombstone's Founder Finds a Resting Place

In 1877, Ed Schieffelin began prospecting in the hills around what was to become Tombstone. Soldiers stationed nearby told him all he would ever find was his own tombstone. He soon found an outcropping of silver and the rush was on. Remembering the prophecy of the soldiers, Schieffelin named his claim, and the future town, "Tombstone."

When he died in 1897, he requested to be buried in Tombstone. He is now buried under a monument that is about 25 feet tall and shaped like an old miner's claim - a fitting end to the man who was warned he would only find his own tombstone.
